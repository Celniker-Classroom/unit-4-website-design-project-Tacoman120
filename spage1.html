History

The history of Magic: The Gathering has a complex and deep history filled with innovation, growth, and imagination throughout the years. From it's founding by Richard Garfield in 1993 to now, it has entertained millions of players all over the world.

Time line of important events

Event Date
Richard Garfield begins developing card games in his free time, specifically a game named Five Colors, which he had developed throughout his childhood based on the idea of five elemental magics that were drawn from geographically diverse areas. 1963 - 1985
Richard Garfield develops a game called RoboRally, and begins looking for a publisher 1985
Richard Garfield meets with Peter Adkison, owner of Wizards of the Coast, who rejects his game, but says he likes his ideas, and wants him to develop a portable game mid-1991
While hiking near Multnomah falls, Garfield is struck with the idea to apply his Five Magics idea to collectible color-themed decks, so that each player could make their own deck that was unique to them. He then combines RoboRally and Five Magics to make Magic: The Gathering mid-1991
Garfield meets with Adkison again within the week and pitches the idea to him. Adkison loves it, seeing how it would be possible to expand upon the game indeffinetly in the future, and having the possibility of making millions. mid-1991
Six months after Garfield originally meeted with Adkison, he has finished the first version of the game. He set the narrative in "Dominia", a multiverse, which allows for the many cards and creatures that exist in Magic. He says he was most influenced by Cosmic Encounter, from which he took the rule "Whenever a card's text directly contradicts the game's rules, the card takes precidence. He was also inspired by dungeons and dragons" 1992
Garfield and Adkison are ready to debut Magic: The Gathering at Gen Con, a gaming convention in Milwaulkee. Adkison drove towards Milwaulkee, stoping at game stores along the way to build support for Gen Con. He met with Garfield at the convention. Their card orders were delayed by a day, but they were still able to sell out of all 2.5 million cards by the end of the convention. August 1993
Wizards have sold out of their supply of 10 million cards four months after their release, and Magic is an instant hit. October 1993
The game begins to release expansions with additional cards and begins selling more and more cards, eventually reaching the one billion cards printed point. 1994
Magic: The Gathering "Pro Tour" is established, with a collection of tournaments where players can win cash prizes over the course of a weekend 1996
Wizards of the coast buys TSR, publisher of Dungeons and Dragons, furthering the volume of games under it's belt. It is then consecutively purchased by Hasbro. 1997-1999
Magic: The Gathering continues to release sets and expansions, increasing the number in recent years and incorperating "Universes Beyond" sets, which are sets that use different IPs. 2000-2026
