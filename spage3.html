Getting Started

Magic: The Gathering (alternatively MTG or Magic) is one of the deepest and most rewarding strategy games ever created—but it can look intimidating at first. The good news is you don't need to understand everything to start enjoying it. A lack of knowledge when playing the game can seem confusing at first, but later becomes fun as you experience new mechanics and other ways of playing the game. However, it is always helpful to have a solid foundation of cards and decks to ensure that you can have the best first experience possible.

The Right Product

Many people see Magic as any other trading card game: buy packs and open them until you get the cards you want, and then build a deck. This is the greatest mistake beginners can make. It is one of the most fun parts of Magic, but is far from the best way to learn or get started. Instead, its recomended to buy one of the products below, which are normally cheap and easily accessible, as all are available on Amazon or at your local game store.

Starter Kits

A starter kit is a beginner box designed so two people can learn and play immediately—no deck-building required. At its core, every starter kit includes: 2 ready-to-play 60-card decks, a learn-to-play guide, deck boxes + tokens, and often codes for the digital game (MTG Arena) They're specifically made to teach you step-by-step while you play. The hardest part of Magic is deckbuilding because of the large sum needed to test over and over. Starter Kits remove that need, making it the fastest way to begin.

Themed Starter

Themed Starters are Starter Kits but with the addition of being part of a Universes Beyond collaboration with a specific franchise. Examples of this include Final Fantasy, Lord of The Rings, and Assassin's Creed. These kits have the same structure (2 decks + guide). They always contain cards themed around a franchise, and are still beginner-friendly, just more collectible and fun. Starter kits like this still include everything needed for two players right out of the box.

Beginners Box

The Beginner Box is more like a full-on learning system + sandbox combined. It doesn't just teach you how to play, it also gradually turns you into someone who understands how Magic works. Inside the box you get 10 mini-decks (called Jumpstart decks), each one having 20 cards and representing a theme (like Vampires, Cats, Goblins, etc.). It has around 200 total cards. It also contains step-by-step tutorial decks (pre-arranged), playmats, life counters, and guides. The most important aspect of the beginners box is you can mix together any 2 mini-decks to create a playable 40 card one, giving you 45 possible deck combinations, making every game unique.

Commander Pre-Cons

A Commander preconstructed deck (pre-con) is a ready-to-play 100-card deck built for the Commander format. You open the box, Shuffle the deck, and play immediately, without any deck-building required. They're made by Wizards of the Coast specifically so players can get started with Commander without needing a collection. A typical precon includes one ready-made 100-card deck, including the commander (your main card), alonside tokens, accessories, and a strategy guide. All the cards, tokens, and the deck box are themed around the commander, as it is the center of your deck.

Learn Through Experience

Magic has thousands of cards, so trying to memorize everything is impossible. Instead, try to play games regularly, learn from your mistakes, and ask questions when you don't understand something. The Magic community is full of people happy to help others learn the game and develope a shared passion for it. Through doing this, you'll naturally start recognizing patterns like when to attack, when to hold back, and how to use your cards effectively. It is also important to develop your own playstyle and find what suits you. Magic has five colors, each with its own themes.

  • White - teamwork, healing, order
  • Blue - control, card draw, counterspells
  • Black - sacrifice, power at a cost
  • Red - speed, direct damage, aggression
  • Green - big creatures, growth, ramping mana

Try a few, or maybe all of them, and see what feels right. Your preferred color often reflects how you like to think and play.

Finding Your Magic Community

Magic is best enjoyed with others. You can play with friends at home, visit a local game store for casual events, or join online communities like Discord groups. You can always find someone happy to help a beginner learn to play. Another important aspect of Magic: The Gathering is your local gamestore. These gamestores are the heart of the Magic community, and provide services from buying and selling single cards or assorted products, hosting tournaments, and creating opprotunites to learn and grow your knowledge about the game. If you are going to buy products or single cards, doing so at your local gamestore makes a big difference, as it helps them grow and keep creating the opprotunites to grow the community.

Final Words

Magic: The Gathering can be as casual or as deep as you want it to be. You don't need to master everything to have fun. Start small, play often, and let your understanding grow naturally. If you stick with it, you'll discover why so many players have been hooked for decades. It's a game that rewards creativity, strategy, and curiosity every time you play, and can turn even sitting down at a table for two hours staring at pieces of cardboard an unforgettable experience.
