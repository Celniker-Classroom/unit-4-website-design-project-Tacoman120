Magic: The Gathering is well known for it's complicated stategy and deckbuilding that make it stand apart from other games. However, it is fairly easy to learn the basic ideas behind it and become a proficient player.

Goal

Reduce the oppenent's life total from the staring twenty to zero. You win if you are the last player standing.

Important Conecpts

Deck

Games of Magic: The Gathering (or, just Magic) are played with decks of cards, varying with their sizes and rules with different game modes. The basic form of Magic, and the first game you will most likely play is the standard format, which is played with a deck of sixty cards, and cards can have up to four copies in a deck.

Turns

The game takes place in turns with each player switching off. Each turn is made up of various "phases", which will be explained later. However, just because it isn't your turn doesn't mean you can't play. Some cards, specifically the "instant" card type, can be played during other's turns, as well as the abilities of cards.

Cards

Magic: The Gathering revolves around the use of cards, which are normally catagorized as many different types. The two main types are Lands and Spells. Spells are almost every card in the game, and cost "mana" to cast. Lands are a type of card whose main function is to generate "mana" to cast spells with. Unless a card says you can, you can only play one land card per turn.

Players

Magic: The Gathering is commonly played with two players in it's standard format, but you can theoretically play with up to six players in other formats. Magic's most popular format, Commander, is designed to be played with four different players. Each player has one deck that they play with and each take one turn, going in a order chosen by the players before the game.

Lands

Land cards are your main source of generating mana to play spells. You can play one per turn, and have special rules. When you use a land, you "tap it", which entails rotating it 90 degrees clockwise or counterclockwise depending on preference. This land is then "taped out", and cannot be used again until the start of the next turn. Spells that say "Counter target spell" cannot counter lands because they are not considered spells.

Creatures

One of the main types of spells are creatures, which are, as the name implies, creatures that you control. You use creatures to attack your opponents and block your opponent's creatures when they attack you. Each creature has a attack and defense stat, which is located in the bottom left of the card in that order. During your combat phase, you can attack your opponents by "taping" your creatures, similar to lands, but you cannot block with a tapped creature. Creatures can die, and when they do, they go to your graveyard, which is essentially your discard pile.

Instants and Socceries

Instants and Socceries are types of spells that resolve as soon as they are played, and don't last. They provide one time effects. After they are resolved, they go strait to the graveyard.

Permanents

Permanents are all cards that stay on the battlefield after they are played. Creatures are considered permanents. Other important types of permantents are Artifacts and Enchantments, which provide lasting effects, but can't attack and block like creatures.

Planeswalkers

Planeswalkers are a very special type of card, which are treated similar to another player. Once during your turn, you can activate one of their abilities. Planeswalkers have a number in their bottom left corner, which is their loyalty. Their loyalty can change based on the abilities you use, but once it reaches zero, they go to the graveyard. Planeswalkers cannot attack or block. When a opponent decides to attack, they can choose to attack you or the Planeswalker. You can use your creatures to deffend the Planeswalker, and any damage done to it will be removed from it's loyalty.

Set Up

At the start of every game, players need to shuffle their decks until the order is random to ensure fairness. They can also cut eachothers decks for confidence that neither of them can cheat. They then need to choose who is going first, most commonly by agreement between players or by dice roll if a agreement can't be reached. It is important to not that whoever goes first is not able to draw on their first turn in most game formats, with the exception of Commander. Finially, each player needs to draw seven cards to make up their starting hand.

Mulligan

If a player does not like their starting hand, they are able "mulligan", which means they suffle it back into the deck and draw a new one. For newer players, it is best to play with a "free mulligan", which means the player is able to draw seven new cards without any consequences. However, in a normal game, when a player draws a mulligan, they draw one card less than their hand before. For example if I didn't like my first hand of seven cards, I would mulligan and draw six new cards instead of seven. If I wanted to mulligan again after that, I would draw five cards, then four, then three, and so on.

Turn Order

Begining Phase

Begining Phase is made up of Untap, Upkeep, and Draw

  1. During Untap, you untap any cards under your control that are tapped, including Lands, Creatures, Artifacts, and Enchantments.
  2. During Upkeep, you resolve any effects of abilities that say "At the beginning of your Upkeep"
  3. During draw, you draw one card from your deck. You can only have seven cards in your hand unless a card says you can have more, or else you will have to discard at the end of your turn.

First Main Phase

During your First Main Phase, you can do two things:

  1. You can play one land card from your hand if you have one
  2. You can activate the abilities of your creatures and cast spells using "mana"

Combat

During Combat, you can use your creatures to attack a player or a Plansewalker. It is made up of various steps.

  1. Declare attackers by tapping the creatures you want to attack your opponent or their Plansewalker with
  2. The opponent will declare blockers, if any, for your attacking creatures
  3. Damage will be dealt to blocking and attacking creatures based on the attack and defense of the card they are blocking or being blocked by. If they recieve more damage than their defense, they die.

Second Main Phase

Same as the first main phase. If you didn't play a land before, you can play one now.

Ending Phase

Until end of turn effects wear off, and you pass the turn to your opponent. Discard down to seven cards in hand if you have more.
